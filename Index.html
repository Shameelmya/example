The Journey

From Humble Beginnings to Global Recognition

Born into a clerical family in the Malabar district of Kerala, Abdul Hakeem Faizy Adrissery's pursuit of knowledge followed an unconventional path. Leaving formal schooling in his early years, he dedicated eight years to traditional Islamic education (Dars) across various mosques.

In 1977, he joined Jamia Nooriya Arabic College in Pattikkad. An avid reader, he continuously sought to bridge the gap between traditional theological training and the demands of the modern world. His academic journey also includes his time at Jannathul Uloom Arabic College in Palakkad.

After serving as an Imam in Abu Dhabi for a decade, he returned to Kerala in the 1990s. Recognizing the necessity for religious scholars to be conversant with modern secular disciplines, he embarked on a mission to completely revolutionize the Islamic education system in India.

Transforming Minds

The Educational Revolution

Pioneering a cross-fertilizing knowledge system that harmonizes deep spiritual learning with contemporary university education.

Coordination of Islamic Colleges (CIC)

Serving as the General Secretary, Faizy formalized the CIC in 2000. It stands as an academic confederation functioning as a private university system, bringing together over 90 affiliated Islamic colleges under a standardized, progressive curriculum.

Wafy & Wafiyya Programs

He architected the highly acclaimed 'Wafy' (for boys) and 'Wafiyya' (for girls) postgraduate programs. These comprehensive courses mandate students to complete UGC-approved university degrees alongside their rigorous Islamic theological studies, producing a new generation of well-rounded scholars.

International Impact

Representing India on the Global Stage

League of Islamic Universities

In 2018, Hakeem Faizy became the first Indian to be nominated and selected to the 21-member executive governing body of the Cairo-based League of Islamic Universities (LIU), acknowledging his successful integrated education model.

World Muslim Communities Council

He serves as an esteemed representative from India at the General Secretariat of the World Muslim Communities Council, headquartered in Abu Dhabi, contributing to global dialogues on community development and education.

Core Philosophy

Empowerment Through Progressive Thought

Women's Educational Empowerment

A fierce advocate for women's higher education, he implemented structural reforms ensuring female students complete their integrated degree courses without interruption, aiming to build a deeply educated and independent generation of women.

Moderate Faith & Pluralism

He is a strong proponent of moderate faith and religious pluralism. He actively promotes an educational framework that equips scholars to engage constructively and harmoniously within diverse, modern societies.
